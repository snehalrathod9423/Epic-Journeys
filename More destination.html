Top Attractions

Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh




Varanasi is one of the oldest living cities in the world and a major spiritual center in India.

Kerala Backwaters, Kerala

The backwaters of Kerala are a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets, offering a tranquil experience amidst lush greenery.

Qutub Minar, Delhi

The Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi. This 73-meter tall minaret, made of red sandstone and marble, is the tallest brick minaret in the world.

Meenakshi Temple, Madurai

The Meenakshi Temple in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, is one of the most important temples in South India, dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Shiva

Jaisalmer Fort, Rajasthan

Jaisalmer Fort, also known as the Golden Fort, is one of the largest fully preserved fortified cities in the world.

Restaurants

Experience the local cuisine at these top-rated restaurants.

Bukhara, New Delhi

Bukhara, located in the ITC Maurya Hotel in New Delhi, is one of India's most iconic restaurants, renowned for its traditional North-West Frontier cuisine.

Indian Accent, New Delhi

Indian Accent, also in New Delhi, is celebrated for its innovative approach to Indian cuisine.

Karavalli, Bengaluru

The menu reflects the rich culinary traditions of the Malabar, Mangalorean, and Goan regions, with an emphasis on fresh seafood and spices.
